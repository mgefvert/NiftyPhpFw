9. Date and time

PHP has in itself classes to work with dates and times, but they are insufficient in providing the rich spectrum of date/time-handling that may be necessary for applications. Common problems with date/time handling that arise are

  • The ability to handle dates only, apart from times and timezones
  • The ability to handle time values only, to measure time regardless of calendar application
  • To specifically give date and time values, along with detailed timezone data.

For this reason, Nifty expands on PHP's datetime handling with three new classes: NF_Date, NF_Time and NF_DateTime.

8.1 NF_Date

NF_Date is a class that stores days in the format "number of days passed since 1899-12-30" (or if it's 1899-12-31?). This is compatible with Excel days and Delphi/Object Pascal dates. This makes it easy to handle date arithmetic, convert to and from other date values, and handle date information without regard to timezones, which is sometimes necessary. For instance, calculating holidays does not pay respect to timezone information - December 24th will always be Christmas Eve, regardless of which timezone you're in.

8.2 NF_Time

NF_Time is a class that handles time only. It stores time internally as seconds, giving it adequate precision to handle most time values. It can be used to calculate both positive and negative time information, and handle time arithmetic. (Joe logs into the system at 6:45am and logs out at 9:30am. How many hours did he work?)

8.3 NF_DateTime

The class NF_DateTime is a combination of these two classes and bypasses some of the normal Unix timestamp handling. This means that it is capable of handling dates from anywhere between Jan 1st, 100 to Dec 31st, 9999.

More will be written on this subject. Especially when we lock in how the Timezone stuff is to be handled. The classes are, however, already excellently capable, and power a lot of different date/time stuff in professional systems.
